This atlas connects optimizer update rules, geometry, evidence, and open
questions. Paper-facing views are built from one validated YAML dataset so that
publication status, source versions, claims, and evidence conditions stay
consistent across the site.

## Start with the map

Use the [Literature Map](/llm-optimizer-atlas/literature-map/) to move through
five research tracks: norms and duality, structured preconditioning,
constrained matrix geometry, benchmarking and scaling, and systems-efficient
optimization.

## Atlas surfaces

- [Paper Library](/llm-optimizer-atlas/paper-notes/) — generated views and one
  durable record per verified paper.
- [Claim–Evidence Matrix](/llm-optimizer-atlas/claim-evidence-matrix/) — claims
  aligned with their evidence and comparison conditions.
- [Open Problems](/llm-optimizer-atlas/open-problems/) — research questions with
  explicit scope and completion criteria.
- [Reading Queue](/llm-optimizer-atlas/reading-queue/) — verified records still
  marked unread.
- [Experiment Ledger](/llm-optimizer-atlas/experiment-notes/) — reproducible
  experiment records.
- [Where Papers Disagree](/llm-optimizer-atlas/where-papers-disagree/) —
  protocol-aware synthesis of conditional conclusions.
- [Benchmark Comparisons](/llm-optimizer-atlas/benchmark-comparisons/) —
  comparable experimental conditions and resource accounting.
- [Watchlist](/llm-optimizer-atlas/watchlist/) — verified frontier preprints
  monitored for source and publication changes.

## Verification contract

Paper metadata, publication status, update rules, URLs, and quantitative
evidence enter the atlas only after primary-source verification. Structured
facts have one source of truth; generated pages are read-only consumers and
never overwrite human-maintained notes.

## Current milestone

The verified seed corpus now drives the paper library, grouped views, reading
queue, frontier watchlist, claim–evidence matrix, and disagreement synthesis.
Open-problem and experiment records remain separate, human-reviewed content
families.
