This atlas is a working surface for connecting optimizer update rules, geometry,
evidence, and open questions. The scaffold contains no verified paper records or
research claims yet.

## Start with the map

Use the [Literature Map](/llm-optimizer-atlas/literature-map/) to move through
five research tracks:
norms and duality, structured preconditioning, constrained matrix geometry,
benchmarking and scaling, and systems-efficient optimization.

## Atlas surfaces

- [Paper Library](/llm-optimizer-atlas/paper-notes/) — one durable note per
  verified paper.
- [Claim–Evidence Matrix](/llm-optimizer-atlas/claim-evidence-matrix/) — claims
  separated from the evidence and conditions that support them.
- [Open Problems](/llm-optimizer-atlas/open-problems/) — research questions with
  explicit scope and completion criteria.
- [Reading Queue](/llm-optimizer-atlas/reading-queue/) — verification work
  waiting to be scheduled.
- [Experiment Ledger](/llm-optimizer-atlas/experiment-notes/) — reproducible
  experiment records.
- [Where Papers Disagree](/llm-optimizer-atlas/where-papers-disagree/) —
  protocol-aware synthesis of conflicting findings.
- [Benchmark Comparisons](/llm-optimizer-atlas/benchmark-comparisons/) —
  comparable experimental conditions and resource accounting.
- [Watchlist](/llm-optimizer-atlas/watchlist/) — leads that are not yet part of
  the verified corpus.

## Verification contract

Paper metadata, publication status, theorem statements, URLs, and quantitative
results enter the atlas only after primary-source verification. Structured facts
will have one source of truth; human interpretation will remain in Markdown or
MDX and will never be overwritten by generation scripts.

## Current milestone

This first milestone establishes navigation, content families, and offline
maintenance checks. Paper schema design and literature verification belong to
later, separately reviewed stages.
